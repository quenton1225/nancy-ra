Issues for Experiment 1A (For Brainstorming)

1. Diagnostic Difficulty
Some types of neurodiversity exist on a spectrum and are not categorical diagnoses.
It is therefore difficult, and potentially not ethically appropriate, for using AI to diagnose or classify all of them.

We may need to adjust what we want to detect. Instead of detecting specific neurodivergent types, we may focus on the three major categories in management studies: Cognitive, emotional, or behavioral patterns. By doing so we can catch Observable functional needs and state-based variations during task performance

2. Types of Neurodiversity (Preliminary List)
According to my knowledge, there are too many types of neurodiversity.

My Thoughts
1.It is impossible for us to identify and focus on all of the above mentioned special needs users. If we select only some to study, it is theoretically difficult to justify why those specific conditions. Even if we include all of them, it would be difficult to provide personalized or type-specific solutions.

The bad news:
Many conditions share similar symptoms.
The good news:
Many conditions share similar symptoms.

 Because of symptom overlap, we may shift toward a symptom-based or function-based support approach.

2.Instead of labeling or identifying neurodivergent types, we may focus on identifying special needs based on observable functional differences. In management research, we often classify constructs into three domains:
•	Cognitive
•	Affective (emotional)
•	Behavioral

Since cognitive and affective/emotional needs are often latent, whereas behavioral signals are relatively easier to detect, our first step should be to explore how AI can infer users’ underlying cognitive and emotional needs from observable behavioral expressions and provide appropriate adaptive support accordingly.
